Biography

Nelson Mandel was the first democratically elected president of South Africa. He is considered to be one of the most influential and inspiring leader of the 20th century for his contribution in fostering peaceful reconciliation by being a forefronter of the anti-aparthied movement.

Nelson was able to reunite very polarised nation through the act of forgiveness rather than seeking retaliation or revenage. He used rugby as a vehicle to overcome the aparthied segregation and racism even though the rugby was symbolised as the sports of Afrikans exclusively. Nelson's gesture of forgiveness by endorsing rugby players, demonstrates that one must transcend from the past legacy instead of acting on fear - to forgive than to avenage. His great leadership was recognised internationally and Nelson was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993.

Impacts on Society

  • Mandela was able to lead a successful transition from aparthied to a multircal democracy.
  • Won the Nobel Prize for Peace in 1993 for playing the leading role in ending the Apartheid.
  • Elected as the first black president of South Africa, serving from 1994 to 1999.
  • Focused on the reconciliation of Afrikans and native South Africans during his presidency
  • Mandela founded the Nelson Mandela Children's Fund to help the orphans of the AIDS crisis in 1995.
  • After his five years of presidency, Mandela continuted to advcoate for causes such as inequality and AIDS crisis in rural parts of South Africa.
